Now, as a criminology student, Jessa spends her days immersed in lectures on criminal law, and late-night study sessions. She thrives on understanding the complexities of crime and justice. Despite her rigorous academic schedule, Jessa has found ways to balance her life. Reading has become one of her favorite pastimes, offering her a way to process her thoughts and explore ideas beyond her coursework. She also enjoys listening to music, which helps her recharge and reflect on her goals. Looking to the future, Jessa is clear about her ambitions. She dreams of a career where she can make a meaningful impact, whether as a forensic analyst, a criminal investigator, or an advocate for justice system reform. For her, criminology isn’t just about solving crimes; it’s about understanding the root causes of wrongdoing and contributing to a fairer and safer society. Jessa, born on a crisp November day in 2003, has come a long way. What began as a childhood curiosity has blossomed into a lifelong passion for uncovering the truth and making a difference.
